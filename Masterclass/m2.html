Module 2: Competitor Research

Competitor Research using Facebook Ad Library

In this video, we’ll go over how to perform competitor research by using the Facebook Ad Library. We’ll look at how businesses similar to ours are using Facebook ads, what’s working for them, and how we can learn from their strategies. Let’s break it down step-by-step.

What is the Facebook Ad Library?

The Facebook Ad Library is a tool that allows you to see all the active ads being run by businesses on Facebook. Here’s how to use it:

Steps for Using the Facebook Ad Library

  1. Search for Facebook Ad Library: Type "Facebook Ad Library" into your browser search bar and click the first result.
  2. Select 'All Ads': Under the ad category section, select “All Ads” to view ads from various businesses.
  3. Use Keywords: Enter your industry or service (e.g., plumbing) to see relevant ads.
  4. Filter by Language: Choose English as your preferred language to filter ads.
  5. Set Date Range: For accurate competitor analysis, filter for ads that have been active for at least three months. Adjust the date range to look at ads from a few months ago, as newly started ads may still be in testing.

Identifying Competitor Ads

Let’s assume you’re in the plumbing business. By searching for “plumbing” in the Facebook Ad Library, you can see a variety of ads that are running in your industry. The goal here is to identify which ads have been running for several months, as these are likely performing well for the businesses behind them.

Analyzing an Example Ad

Here’s an example from J Hood Services, a business offering AC, heating, and plumbing services. Their ad has been running since September 1, 2024, which means it’s been working well enough to be maintained for a long time. This ad’s simple approach is likely a key factor in its success.

The ad features a clear call to action (CTA) and focuses on the HVAC unit, which is easy for customers to understand. Although there are areas to improve (e.g., simplifying the messaging and focusing on one service like HVAC repair), this ad has proven results.

Optimizing Your Own Ads

If you were to run an ad for a similar business, you could aim for simplicity. The key takeaway here is to make the message clear and concise—focus on one core service, use an effective image, and include a simple call to action.

Why Simple Ads Work

From experience, keeping ads as simple as possible tends to work best, especially for local service businesses. While high production ads (like videos or flashy creatives) can be great for large-scale campaigns, they are not necessary for smaller businesses with limited ad budgets. In fact, sometimes less is more, and a simple ad can have a higher return on investment (ROI).

Pro Tip:

It's important to understand that ads are about solving problems for your audience. People needing plumbing or HVAC services are looking for practical, timely solutions—not flashy entertainment.

Exploring Other Examples

Another example is Affordable Building Solutions, which has been running ads since August 2024. Their ad is straightforward, featuring simple stock images of houses. This is another example of a low-budget, effective ad that focuses on utility.

Other industries, like personal injury lawyers or med spas, also use simple ads. For example, one law firm uses a straightforward image of a truck accident with the headline: "After a truck accident, contact an expert." This clear, niche-focused messaging resonates with the target audience.

Video Ads vs. Image Ads

Video ads can be effective, but they’re not always necessary. For certain services like law firms or med spas, a quick and simple image ad often performs better. Video ads are typically used for building awareness or top-of-funnel marketing, but they may not be cost-effective for smaller businesses.

Google Ad Library

While the Facebook Ad Library is user-friendly, the Google Ad Library is a bit more complex. You typically need to know the business or website you want to research. However, Google’s ad library is still a valuable tool for competitive research, especially if you're focusing on local markets or niche services.

Example: Yoga Studio Ad

One example from Google Ad Library shows a simple ad for a yoga studio, promoting a new student special. This is another example of a highly focused ad targeting a specific audience (new students) with a clear, simple offer.

Conclusion

By using the Facebook Ad Library (and Google Ad Library), you can gain valuable insights into your competitors’ advertising strategies. This research allows you to create more effective ads, improve your targeting, and ultimately, increase your conversions. Remember, simple, clear, and focused ads are often the most effective, especially for local businesses. By studying the ads that are working in your industry, you can replicate their success and optimize your own campaigns for better results.
