Module 3: Google Analytics

Setting Up a Google Analytics Account

In this video, I will walk you through how to set up a Google Analytics account and start measuring your website. It's a quick and easy process—let’s break it down step-by-step.

What is Google Analytics?

Google Analytics is a tool provided by Google that allows you to track and analyze the performance of your website. Here's what it does:

  • Tracks User Behavior: It monitors actions such as page views, clicks, and interactions on your website.
  • Provides Insights: It collects data about your website visitors and sends it to Google Analytics for analysis.
  • Helps with Marketing Decisions: With this data, you can adjust your marketing efforts to focus on what’s working best.

Key Takeaway:

No Data, No Insights: Without setting up Google Analytics, you won’t be able to track your website’s performance or make data-driven marketing decisions.

Where to Start: Setting Up Your Google Analytics Account

Follow these steps to get started with Google Analytics:

  1. Go to Google Analytics: Visit Google Analytics.
  2. Sign in with Your Google Account: Log in to Google Analytics using your Google account credentials.
  3. Create a New Account: You’ll be prompted to create a new account for your website. This is like an umbrella for multiple properties or websites.
  4. Set Up Your Account: Enter your account name (e.g., "Personal Websites") and configure the data-sharing settings as needed.

Configuring Your Property

Now we need to create a property in Google Analytics for your website:

  1. Property Name: Enter the name of your website or brand (e.g., "My Personal Website").
  2. Time Zone & Currency: Set your reporting time zone and currency. This is important for accurate reporting.
  3. Advanced Settings: Don’t worry about the advanced settings; Universal Analytics will stop collecting data in July 2023. Just skip this section for now.
  4. Business Information: Choose your business category and size. This step is mostly for benchmarking and helping with Google Ads.
  5. Business Objectives: Select your website’s goals, whether it's generating leads, e-commerce, brand awareness, or user behavior analysis.

Accepting Terms and Setting Up Tracking

After setting up your property, you need to accept the terms of service:

  1. Accept the Terms: Choose your country and agree to the terms of service.
  2. Add Tracking Code: Google Analytics will give you a tracking code to add to your website. You can either install it manually or use a CMS integration.

Manually Adding the Tracking Code

If you're adding the code manually, follow these steps:

  1. Copy the tracking code provided by Google Analytics.
  2. Paste it into the <head> section of your website’s HTML.
  3. Save your changes and publish the updates to your site.

Installing Google Analytics Using a CMS (e.g., WordPress)

If you're using a platform like WordPress, you can simplify the installation:

  1. Install SiteKit Plugin: From your WordPress dashboard, go to Plugins > Add New and search for "SiteKit by Google."
  2. Activate SiteKit: Install and activate the plugin.
  3. Connect to Google Analytics: Use the SiteKit setup wizard to connect your Google Analytics account to your WordPress website.
  4. Verify & Complete: Follow the on-screen instructions to verify the setup, and you're done!

Conclusion

Once you’ve set up Google Analytics, you’ll start tracking valuable user behavior data on your website. This will help you make informed decisions about your marketing efforts and improve the performance of your site. If you follow these steps, you’ll be on your way to getting the most out of Google Analytics!
